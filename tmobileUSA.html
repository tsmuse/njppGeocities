T-Mobile USA

Date Completed: 2012

Project Type: Mobile & Enterprise desktop apps

Solved: Information architecture, Visual design, Interaction design, Usability testing, User research

Live at: internal applications

The Project

T-Mobile brought me in to work on both the current and next generation of their QuikView software. QuikView is T-Mobile’s internal, enterprise, desktop customer support software. It’s used by the approximately 10,000 call center reps and 2,000 retail store reps to research, manage and create T-Mobile customer accounts and lines of service in the United States.

Work in my time at T-Mobile included bringing the QuikView desktop application to a tablet platform for use in stores, as well as numerous improvements to the existing desktop application. I lead the design efforts to bring the application to the tablet platform for use in the retail stores. This included running observational studies in the stores as well as working with our research team to set up interviews and surveys. This research lead me to make many crucial design decisions in the tablet application, including UI orientation based on studies of how the representatives used their hands while they were working.My work later in the contract period included leading design efforts on improvements to the existing desktop application with a larger team.
